# Taste axes (signal checklist)

Use these axes to describe patterns across *liked* vs *disliked* movies without overfitting to one title.

## Tone

- **Optimism**: hopeful/comforting ↔ bleak/cynical
- **Sincerity**: earnest ↔ ironic/snarky
- **Emotional intensity**: gentle ↔ emotionally punishing
- **Humor**: dry/subtle ↔ broad/slapstick ↔ none

## Pacing & density

- **Tempo**: slow burn ↔ brisk
- **Scene density**: hangout/atmospheric ↔ plot-dense
- **Clarity**: straightforward ↔ requires active attention

## Narrative shape

- **Resolution**: tidy endings ↔ ambiguity tolerated/desired
- **Structure**: linear ↔ nonlinear/fractured
- **Driver**: character evolution ↔ mystery/twists ↔ set pieces

## World & realism

- **Realism**: grounded ↔ heightened/stylized
- **Worldbuilding**: minimal ↔ lore-heavy
- **Genre bending**: pure genre ↔ hybrid/experimental

## Theme preferences

Look for repeated “theme triggers” (positive or negative):

- authority vs rebellion
- family/relationships
- moral ambiguity and ethical dilemmas
- identity and belonging
- justice/revenge/redemption
- social critique

## Craft / “movie-ness”

- **Performance-first**: acting and relationships drive enjoyment
- **Direction/visuals**: composition, color, camera movement matter a lot
- **Sound/music**: score-heavy vs restrained
- **Dialogue**: sharp writing ↔ minimal dialogue

## Common dealbreaker buckets

Use dislikes to identify which of these are hard nos vs soft nos:

- “contrived” plotting / coincidences
- tonal whiplash
- excessive cruelty or gore
- cringe humor / secondhand embarrassment
- messy exposition / lore dumps
- shallow characterization
